Rudolf Steiner School

My school's curriculum embodies a unique learning philosophy called the Waldorf curriculum. I have been going to the Rudolf Steiner School since nursery, so the Waldorf curriculum has played a significant role in both my academic and personal development. Most importantly, it has exposed me to a strong balance of the arts and sciences, and has taught me how to think, not what to think.

Main Lesson Book

The Main Lesson Book is an integral part of the Waldorf curriculum. It is a collection of course material (essays, drawings, etc.) that each student puts together as the culmination of a three to four week Main Lesson. In other words, instead of learning from textbooks, we create our own.

Junior Author's Paper

The Junior Author's Paper is a collection of essays on the life and works of an author. I chose Agatha Christie, the renowned British mystery writer. The paper is divided into three parts:
Part I – a biography of my chosen author
Part II – my analysis on three of her works
Part III – a collection of literary criticisms of her work
